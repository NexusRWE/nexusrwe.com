Use Case 2: A Hospital Participates in RWE

Without Exporting Patient Data

The situation

A large academic medical center has 1.2 million patient records and a capable clinical informatics team. Pharma sponsors and CROs frequently approach about data partnerships. The CIO consistently declines. HIPAA exposure, anti-kickback statute concerns, breach risk, and reputational risk all outweigh the modest payments offered.

The conventional model

Sign a data use agreement. Export a de-identified extract to a sponsor or aggregator. Hope the de-identification holds. Accept ongoing legal exposure for years. Get paid as a function of how many patient records left the building.

The NexusRWE model

Onboard once with a standard institutional governance configuration. Sponsors submit code-signed query artifacts; the hospital approves or declines on a per-study basis. Approved queries run locally inside an attested sandbox. Only aggregate, differentially private results return; nothing else leaves. The hospital is compensated for compute cycles consumed, not for patient records.

The outcome

The hospital can participate in regulatory-grade studies it previously declined. Data sovereignty is structural, not promised. Compensation is tied to a service the hospital actually provides, not to the volume of patient data exported. The institution stays in control.
